Are Koreans Dying out?

As the world has just recently reached 8 billion population, the population growth has been slower than what many experts predicted years ago. This happens due to many countries started to have much lower birth rate than expected. With South Korea having the lowest birth rate in the world, it already started showing population decrease in its country.

On average, 2 birth rate per woman is needed to maintain a stable population
A birth rate lower than 2 would cause population decrease
and a birth rate higher than 2 would cause population increase

With the current population around 51 million, it is estimated that by 2100, the Korean population will shrink back to what it was in the 1950s, around 24 million.

With Korea's current birth rate, 100 Koreans would be replaced by 55 Koreans by the 2nd generation, and 30 Koreans by the 3rd generation, and eventually the 100 Koreans would be replaced by only 16 Koreans by the 4th generation.

Where is the problem?

The problem is not that there are fewer Koreans, the problem is the age composition of the Koreans. During 1950s, the median age of Korea was 15-19 years old. By 2040, it is estimated to be 50-54 years old. Korea is going to be a country filled with seniors.

This would lead to problems such as, decrease in working population, increase in healthcare cost, increase in dependency ratio, and changes to economy
